✦ Content OS

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 3, 2026

Content OS ("Content OS," "we," "us") is a marketing-intelligence tool for mobile-service businesses. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have. We only access data you explicitly authorize, and we use it solely to provide the service to you.

1. Information we collect

  • Account information you provide when you sign up — your email address and display name (authentication is handled by Google Firebase Authentication).
  • Connected-platform data, only after you authorize a connection via that platform's official OAuth consent screen:
    • TikTok — your profile name/avatar, follower and like counts, and your own videos' public metrics (views, likes, comments, shares), via the TikTok Login Kit and Display API. With the Content Posting API (if enabled and approved), we publish videos you choose to your own account.
    • Google Search Console — your site's search metrics (clicks, impressions, position, queries).
    • Google Business Profile — your profile's performance metrics and reviews; and, if enabled, local posts you create.
    • Mobile Service OS — your business's job and revenue records, read-only, as the signed-in user.
  • Authorization tokens for the platforms you connect, stored securely server-side (see Security). These are never exposed to your browser or to other users.
  • Content you create in the app (captions, posts, notes) and basic usage data needed to operate the product.

2. How we use information

To provide and improve the service for you: to display your performance dashboards and revenue analytics, generate content recommendations and pre-publish scores, and — only when you take that action — to publish your own content to your own connected accounts. We do not sell your data, and we do not use it for advertising.

3. TikTok data

We access TikTok information only through TikTok's official APIs and only with your consent. We use it to show you analytics about your own account and to publish your own content at your direction. We do not share TikTok data with third parties, do not use it to build profiles for advertising, and you can revoke our access at any time (see Your choices). Our use of TikTok data complies with the TikTok Developer Terms.

4. Google user data (Limited Use)

Content OS's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including its Limited Use requirements. We use Google Business Profile and Search Console data only to provide user-facing features in the app; we do not sell it, use it for ads, or allow humans to read it except as needed for support with your consent, for security, or to comply with law.

5. Storage & security

Data is stored in Google Cloud / Firebase (Firestore). Traffic is encrypted in transit. OAuth refresh tokens live in access-restricted server-side storage reachable only by our backend functions — never by the browser and never by other tenants. Each business's data is isolated to its own tenant.

6. How we share information

We do not sell your information. We share it only with infrastructure providers that operate the service (Google Cloud / Firebase), and only as needed to run it, or when required by law.

7. Retention & deletion

Disconnecting a platform deletes its stored authorization token and clears its synced data. You can request deletion of your account and associated data at any time by contacting us; we will delete it within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.

8. Your choices

  • Disconnect any platform inside the app at any time.
  • Revoke our access directly at the platform (e.g., TikTok app settings, your Google Account permissions).
  • Request access to, correction of, or deletion of your data via the contact below.

9. Children

Content OS is a business tool not directed to children and is not intended for anyone under 16.

10. Changes

We may update this policy; we will revise the "Last updated" date above and, for material changes, provide notice in the app.
